About Ede

Ever since I was a little girl, I have found so much joy through dancing. Whether it is competitive dance or country line dancing, I LOVE to dance. In fact, my nickname is Miss Happy Feet since I am always moving my feet.

I am the loudest and proudest member of the fightin’ Texas Aggies class of 2023! I will be receiving my bachelor’s degree in Health with a concentration in Allied Health. The reason I chose this degree is for the purpose of building a strong foundation for optometry school. In approximately eight years I will have the title as a Doctor of Optometry. I chose this profession for the purpose of the mission God has put on my heart to establish a clinic that will improve the Zambian natives’ vision, and the inspiration from Jesus healing the blind. I want people to see God and the beauty of the world around them.

About Luke

My priorities growing up were God, family, school, and sports. I pushed myself academically and in the sports I played: football, baseball, and basketball. Once I reached high school, I played football and raced in triathlons. In high school, I also found a love for building things, especially projects that included LED's.

I was born bleeding maroon and thus became a student at Texas A&M University. I am a computer science major with a minor in embedded systems integration. I chose this path to pursue more complicated projects and learn how computers work from the ground up. I will use this degree to serve underdeveloped countries by advancing their knowledge and use of technology. Before that mission, I will serve in the United States Marine Corps. I am a candidate in the Marine Officers Program in College Station and will attend Officers Candidates School in Quantico, Virginia during the summers of 2021 and 2022. I will commission once I graduate in December 2022 and serve the country that has blessed me dearly.
